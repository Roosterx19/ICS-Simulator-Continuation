# ICS Glossary (Selective)

Key terms that appear in the simulator UI, forms, tooltips, and scenarios. Source: FEMA 0305 Type 3 IMT Appendix E, ICS 400 glossary. Where FEMA has an authoritative definition, use it verbatim — do not paraphrase operational definitions.

**This is selective, not exhaustive.** For the full glossary, see `/mnt/project/0305_Slideshows_Pt_11.pdf` and `/mnt/project/0305_Slideshows_Pt_12.pdf`. If a term you need isn't here, check those before inventing one.

---

**Agency Representative (AREP)** — Assigned person from an agency who has been delegated authority to make decisions on matters affecting that agency's participation at the incident. Reports to the Liaison Officer.

**Area Command** — Organization established to oversee the management of multiple incidents that are each being handled by separate ICS organizations, or to oversee the management of a very large or evolving incident that has multiple IMTs engaged. Does not have operational responsibilities.

**Base** — Location at which primary Logistics functions for an incident are coordinated and administered. There is only ONE Base per incident.

**Branch** — Organizational level having functional or geographical responsibility for major aspects of incident operations. A Branch is between the Section and the Division/Group in Operations, and between the Section and Units in Logistics.

**Camp** — Geographical site within the general incident area, separate from the Incident Base, equipped and staffed to provide food, water, and sanitary services to incident personnel.

**Chain of Command** — Orderly line of authority within the ranks of the incident management organization.

**Check-In** — Process whereby resources first report to an incident. Locations: Incident Command Post, Resources Unit, Base, Camps, Staging Areas, Helibases, Helispots, and Division Supervisors (for direct line assignments).

**Command Staff** — Staff who report directly to the Incident Commander: Public Information Officer, Safety Officer, and Liaison Officer. They may have Assistants.

**Compact** — Formal, typically binding, working agreement between two or more jurisdictions.

**Demobilization** — Orderly, safe, and efficient return of an incident resource to its original location and status.

**Deputy** — Fully qualified individual who, in the absence of a superior, can be delegated the authority to manage a functional operation or perform a specific task.

**Division** — Organizational level responsible for operations within a defined **geographic** area. Between Branch and Single Resources in Operations.

**Emergency Operations Center (EOC)** — Physical location at which the coordination of information and resources to support incident management activities normally takes place. Multi-agency / multi-jurisdictional coordination hub. **Not part of the incident command organization.**

**General Staff** — Group of incident management personnel reporting to the Incident Commander: Operations, Planning, Logistics, Finance/Admin Section Chiefs (and Intel/Investigations SC when established).

**Group** — Established to divide the incident management structure into functional areas of operation. Between Branch and Single Resources in Operations. A Group is the **functional** counterpart to a Division (geographic).

**IAP (Incident Action Plan)** — Oral or written plan containing objectives reflecting the overall incident strategy, tactics, risk management, and member safety for a specific operational period.

**Incident** — Occurrence or event, natural or human-caused, that requires a response to protect life or property.

**Incident Command Post (ICP)** — Field location where the primary tactical-level, on-scene incident command functions are performed.

**Incident Command System (ICS)** — Standardized on-scene incident management concept designed specifically to allow responders to adopt an integrated organizational structure equal to the complexity and demands of any single incident or multiple incidents without being hindered by jurisdictional boundaries.

**Incident Commander (IC)** — Individual responsible for all incident activities, including strategies and tactics and the ordering and release of resources. Has overall authority and responsibility for conducting incident operations.

**Incident Complex** — Two or more individual incidents located in the same general area and assigned to a single IC or Unified Command. (Distinct from a single "complex incident.")

**Incident Complexity** — Incident criteria determined by the level of difficulty, severity, or overall resistance faced by incident management while managing or supporting an incident.

**Incident Management Team (IMT)** — Incident Commander and appropriate Command and General Staff assigned to an incident.

**Initial Response** — Resources initially committed to an incident.

**Joint Information Center (JIC)** — Facility established to coordinate all incident-related public information activities. Central point of contact for all news media.

**Joint Information System (JIS)** — Structure that integrates incident information and public affairs into a cohesive organization designed to provide consistent, coordinated, accurate, accessible, timely, and complete information during crisis.

**Liaison Officer (LOFR)** — Member of the Command Staff responsible for coordinating with representatives from cooperating and assisting agencies or organizations.

**Logistics Section** — Section responsible for providing facilities, services, and material support for the incident.

**MAC Group (Multi-Agency Coordination Group)** — Group of administrators or executives, or their appointed representatives, who are typically authorized to commit agency resources and funds. Policy-level, not operational.

**Management by Objectives** — Incident management approach that involves establishing specific, measurable objectives; identifying strategies, tactics, tasks; and documenting results against the objectives.

**Mobilization** — Process and procedures used by all organizations for activating, assembling, and transporting all resources that have been requested.

**Modular Organization** — ICS and EOC organizational structures develop in a modular fashion based on incident size, complexity, and hazard environment.

**Mutual Aid Agreement** — Written agreement between agencies and/or jurisdictions to assist one another on request.

**NIMS (National Incident Management System)** — Nationwide approach for federal, state, local, and tribal governments; the private sector; and NGOs to work together to prepare for, respond to, and recover from domestic incidents.

**Officer** — The ICS title for personnel responsible for the Command Staff positions of Safety, Liaison, and Public Information.

**Operational Period** — Period of time scheduled for execution of a given set of operation actions as specified in the IAP. Usually not over 24 hours.

**Operations Section** — Section responsible for all tactical operations at the incident.

**Planning Section** — Section responsible for collection, evaluation, and dissemination of incident information and preparation/documentation of IAPs. Maintains current and forecasted situation and resource status.

**Preparedness** — Range of deliberate tasks and activities necessary to build, sustain, and improve the operational capability to prevent, protect against, respond to, and recover from domestic incidents.

**Prevention** — Actions to avoid an incident or to intervene to stop an incident from occurring.

**Resources** — Personnel and major items of equipment, supplies, and facilities available or potentially available for assignment to incident operations.

**Resource Management** — Systems for identifying available resources at all jurisdictional levels to enable timely, efficient, and unimpeded access.

**Resource Typing** — Categorizing resources by capability. Types I through V, with Type I being the most capable.

**Safety Officer (SOFR)** — Member of the Command Staff responsible for monitoring incident operations and advising the IC on all matters relating to operational safety, including the health and safety of emergency responder personnel.

**Section** — Organizational level with responsibility for a major functional area of incident or EOC management (e.g., Operations, Planning, Logistics).

**Single Resource** — Individual, a piece of equipment and its personnel complement, or a crew with an identified work supervisor.

**Situation Unit** — Functional unit within the Planning Section responsible for the collection, organization, and analysis of incident status information.

**Span of Control** — The number of individuals a supervisor is responsible for, usually between 1:3 and 1:7 under NIMS.

**Staging Area** — Location where resources are placed while awaiting a tactical assignment. The Operations Section manages Staging Areas.

**Strategy** — General direction selected to accomplish incident objectives set by the Incident Commander.

**Strike Team** — Specified combination of the **same kind and type** of resources with common communications and a leader.

**Task Force** — Combination of **mixed** resources with common communications and a leader, temporarily assembled for a specific mission.

**Technical Specialist (THSP)** — Personnel with special skills that can be used anywhere within the ICS organization.

**Transfer of Command** — Process of moving the responsibility for incident command from one IC to another.

**Unified Command** — Application of ICS used when there is more than one agency with incident jurisdiction or when incidents cross political jurisdictions. Agencies work together through their designated ICs at a single ICP to establish a common set of objectives and strategies and a single IAP.

**Unit** — Organizational element having functional responsibility for a specific incident Planning, Logistics, or Finance/Administration activity.

**Unity of Command** — Principle that each individual involved in incident operations will be assigned to only one supervisor.
