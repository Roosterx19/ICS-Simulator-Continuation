# Emergency Support Functions (ESFs) & Recovery Support Functions (RSFs)

**Source:** FEMA ICS 400 Unit 5 Handout 5-3 + Unit 5 Visuals 5.37–5.42. Extracted from `/mnt/project/ICS400_SLideshow.pdf`.

ESFs and RSFs are how federal response and recovery capabilities plug into an incident. They're not ICS positions — they're *functional bundles* of agencies and resources that deliver specific **Core Capabilities** from the National Preparedness Goal.

---

## How ESFs Work (short version)

From ICS 400 Unit 5:

> *"Emergency Support Functions (ESFs) may be selectively activated for both Stafford Act and non-Stafford Act incidents. Not all incidents requiring Federal support result in the activation of ESFs. For Stafford Act incidents, the National Response Coordination Center (NRCC) or Regional Response Coordination Center (RRCC) may activate specific ESFs or other Federal agencies (OFAs) by directing appropriate departments and agencies to initiate the actions delineated in the ESF Annexes."*
>
> *"Resources coordinated through ESFs are assigned where needed within the response structure. For example, if a State requests assistance with a mass evacuation, resources from several different ESFs may be integrated into a single Branch or Group within the Operations Section. During the response, these resources would report to a supervisor within the assigned Branch or Group."*

**Key rule:** Once an ESF's resources are integrated into the incident's Operations Section, they follow the IC's chain of command. ESFs coordinate *supply*, not *command*.

## Per-ESF Detail (from ICS 400 Handout 5-3)

### ESF-1 — Transportation
Coordinates transportation systems/infrastructure support, transportation regulation, airspace management, and transportation safety and security.
- Transportation modes management and control
- Transportation safety
- Stabilization and reestablishment of transportation infrastructure
- Movement restrictions
- Damage and impact assessment

### ESF-2 — Communications
Coordinates government and industry efforts for reestablishing critical communications infrastructure, facilitates cyber-event stabilization, and coordinates communications support to response efforts.
- Coordination with telecom and IT industries
- Critical communications infrastructure restoration
- Protection, reestablishment, and sustainment of national cyber and IT resources
- Oversight of communications within Federal response structures
- Facilitation of stabilization of systems and applications from cyber events

### ESF-3 — Public Works and Engineering
Coordinates capabilities to deliver services, technical assistance, engineering expertise, construction management, and related support.
- Infrastructure protection and emergency repair
- Critical infrastructure reestablishment
- Engineering services and construction management
- Emergency contracting support for lifesaving and life-sustaining services

### ESF-4 — Firefighting
Coordinates detection and suppression of fires.
- Support to wildland, rural, and urban firefighting operations

### ESF-5 — Information and Planning
Supports and facilitates multiagency planning and coordination for operations involving incidents requiring Federal coordination.
- Incident action planning
- Information collection, analysis, and dissemination

### ESF-6 — Mass Care, Emergency Assistance, Housing and Human Services
Coordinates delivery of mass care and emergency assistance.
- Mass care
- Emergency assistance
- Temporary housing
- Human services

### ESF-7 — Logistics
Coordinates comprehensive incident resource planning, management, and sustainment capability for survivors and responders.
- Comprehensive national incident logistics planning, management, and sustainment
- Resource support (facility space, office equipment/supplies, contracting services)

### ESF-8 — Public Health and Medical Services
Coordinates mechanisms for assistance in response to actual or potential public health / medical incidents.
- Public health
- Medical surge support including patient movement
- Behavioral health services
- Mass fatality management

### ESF-9 — Search and Rescue
Coordinates rapid deployment of SAR resources.
- Structural collapse (urban) search and rescue
- Maritime / coastal / waterborne search and rescue
- Land search and rescue

### ESF-10 — Oil and Hazardous Materials Response
Coordinates support in response to discharge/release of oil or hazmat.
- Environmental assessment of contamination
- Environmental decontamination and cleanup, including buildings/structures and contaminated waste management

### ESF-11 — Agriculture and Natural Resources
Protects the Nation's food supply, responds to plant and animal pest/disease, protects natural and cultural resources.
- Nutrition assistance
- Animal and agricultural health issue response
- Technical expertise, coordination, support of animal/agricultural emergency management
- Meat, poultry, processed egg products safety and defense
- Natural and cultural resources and historic properties protection

### ESF-12 — Energy
Facilitates reestablishment of damaged energy systems and provides technical expertise during radiological/nuclear incidents.
- Energy infrastructure assessment, repair, and reestablishment
- Energy industry utilities coordination
- Energy forecast

### ESF-13 — Public Safety and Security
Coordinates public safety and security capabilities.
- Facility and resource security
- Security planning and technical resource assistance
- Public safety and security support
- Support to access, traffic, and crowd control

### ESF-15 — External Affairs
Coordinates release of accurate, coordinated, timely, and accessible public information.
- Public affairs and the Joint Information Center
- Intergovernmental (local, state, tribal, territorial) affairs
- Congressional affairs
- Private sector outreach
- All Hazards Emergency Response Operations Tribal

## Recovery Support Functions (RSFs)

From ICS 400 Unit 5 Handout 5-4. RSFs are the recovery counterpart to ESFs, organized under the **National Disaster Recovery Framework (NDRF)**.

**Purpose:** Support local governments by facilitating problem solving, improving access to resources, and fostering coordination among state and federal agencies, nongovernmental partners, and stakeholders.

### The 6 RSFs

| RSF | Coordinating Agency |
|---|---|
| **Community Planning and Capacity Building** | DHS/FEMA |
| **Economic Recovery** | Dept. of Commerce |
| **Health and Social Services** | Dept. of Health and Human Services |
| **Housing** | Dept. of Housing and Urban Development |
| **Infrastructure Systems** | U.S. Army Corps of Engineers |
| **Natural and Cultural Resources** | Dept. of the Interior |

### RSF Summaries

**Community Planning and Capacity Building** — Unifies Federal and nongovernmental expertise to aid local/tribal governments in planning and managing recovery and engaging the whole community.

**Economic Recovery** — Returns economic and business activities (including agricultural) to health. Integrates Federal expertise to help all levels of government and private sector sustain/rebuild businesses, employment, and develop economic opportunities.

**Health and Social Services** — Supports locally led recovery efforts addressing public health, healthcare facilities/coalitions, and essential social service needs. Covers at-risk/vulnerable children, individuals, families.

**Housing** — Coordinates Federal resources for housing solutions. Housing is critical and challenging — must be adequate, affordable, accessible.

**Infrastructure Systems** — Efficiently facilitates restoration of infrastructure systems and services. Improves resilience and future protection.

**Natural and Cultural Resources** — Integrates Federal capabilities to support protection of natural/cultural resources and historic properties through response and recovery actions consistent with post-disaster community priorities and applicable laws (NEPA, ESA, Historic Preservation Act, etc.).

## How ESFs/RSFs Map to the Simulator's Resource Catalog

The resource catalog in `resources` table (Phase 2) should have an `esf_code` or `rsf_code` column so that when the Planning Section plans for a capability, they can filter by "what's available under ESF-4 (Firefighting)?" or "what does ESF-8 cover for mass fatality?"

**Suggested schema addition (Phase 2 migration):**

```sql
alter table public.resources
  add column esf_code text references public.esfs(code),
  add column rsf_code text references public.rsfs(code);

create table public.esfs (
  code text primary key,       -- 'ESF-1' .. 'ESF-15'
  name text not null,
  coordinator text not null,
  core_capabilities text[] not null,
  description text
);

create table public.rsfs (
  code text primary key,       -- 'RSF-CPCB' etc.
  name text not null,
  coordinating_agency text not null,
  description text
);
```

**Seed data for `esfs` and `rsfs`** should come directly from this file's tables. When ready to seed, Claude Code can produce `supabase/seed_esf_rsf.sql` from the data here.

## Capstone Scenario Alignment (ICS 400 Unit 5)

The ICS 400 Capstone Activity directs students to use the *full range* of NIMS command and coordination functional groups to organize a disaster. The simulator's Phase 2/3 "ICS 400 Final" scenario should activate:

- **Full Area Command structure** (multiple ICs under one Area Commander)
- **At least one JFO** with Unified Coordination Group
- **At least 5 ESFs** (e.g. ESF-1, ESF-4, ESF-6, ESF-8, ESF-13 for an urban disaster)
- **MAC Group** active for inter-incident resource prioritization
- **JIC** for multi-agency public information

Completion criterion: student roles map correctly to their functional group and the IAP reflects the coordination structure.
