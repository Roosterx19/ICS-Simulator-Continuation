# Central City Flood Scenario (ICS 300 Unit 2)

**Source:** `ICS_300_AAM_CCFS_Unit-2_maps.pdf` — FEMA ICS 300 Unit 2, April 2019 (AAM-165 through AAM-172)

**Objective:** Apply key Unified Command principles.

---

## Historical Background

The city has a history of being susceptible to flooding. The geology in Liberty County consists of nearly level, somewhat poorly drained, expansive soils on flood plains. The topsoil rests on sandy and silty loam. Runoff is very slow, and the soil is subject to flooding. Permeability is moderate.

**Frequency:** Moderate flooding at least once a year; major flooding generally once every five years.

**Historical event:** A severe flood in 1997 killed 28 people, injured 656, and caused the evacuation of 75,000. It also heavily damaged 377 permanent homes, 65 businesses, and completely destroyed 203 mobile homes.

**Controllability:** Flood damage control is limited to land-use management and elevation criteria. Clearance of debris along stream ways can also affect flooding. Snow runoff and ice damming are not considered major contributors.

**Duration:** Several hours to several days.

**Scope of damage:** Ranges from minimal to nearly total destruction of community facilities, businesses, or residences.

**Intensity of impact:** Ranges from a few houses to several hundred, may include road and utility washouts and bridge damage.

### Dam-Break Flooding

Catastrophic failure of **East Lake Dam** could impact Liberty County and Central City. Floods from the **East Lake River**, **Roaring River**, **Swatera Creek**, and **Turtle River** could impact communities along their banks following failure of the East Lake Dam. If failure occurs during a period of heavy rains, all four waterways could be impacted, and flooding could occur along their banks.

---

## Scenario

For the past three days, it has been raining heavily in Central City and Liberty County, averaging **2.5 inches of rain each 24-hour period**. Central City is divided by the banks of the **Roaring River**, and **Swatera Creek** is a tributary through the northwest part of the city.

In an effort to avoid some of the damage and loss of life caused by previous incidents, the newly elected mayor of Central City has taken an active interest in planning for and responding to flooding disasters. The mayor has asked you to prepare a **staffing plan for the response to anticipated flooding**, in the event that the rains do not abate.

## Instructor Tasks (what the sim replaces)

The original class activity (groups of students, 30 minutes):

1. Review the historical background, scenario, maps, and current resources
2. Identify stakeholders
3. Identify assisting and cooperating agencies
4. Determine whether this is Single or Unified Command
5. If Single — who is IC and why
6. If Unified — who are the members and why
7. Draw an Organization Chart for the IC or UC
8. Identify challenges and safety issues
9. Present rationale to the class

**Simulator equivalent flow:**
1. Student joins session tagged with this scenario
2. Pre-brief modal presents Historical Background + Scenario + map browser of z04/z15/z16/z17
3. Student picks role from the modular board (if instructor has set Unified Command, multiple IC slots open)
4. Student proceeds through ICS-201 (Incident Briefing) with pre-populated Current Resources list
5. Transfer-of-command flow available if the incident complexity escalates (East Lake Dam breach event can be instructor-injected)

## Teaching points this scenario exercises

- **Unified Command** — Central City (city) + Liberty County (county) + State of Columbia (state). If the Roaring River Tribal Community is affected, add tribal UC member. If East Lake Dam fails and multi-county, escalate to Area Command.
- **Modular organization** — scaling from local response → adding Planning Section → adding Logistics branches for sheltering → Finance for emergency contracting
- **Chain of command / Unity of command** — multiple public works crews from city + county must have one supervisor each
- **Facility types** — EOC (Liberty County), ICP (location TBD by IC on map), Base (likely Fairgrounds per z09 — fairgrounds are classic Base sites), Staging (likely a large parking lot near the incident perimeter), Shelter locations (schools, libraries)
- **Resource typing** — Type II vs Type III IMTs, swiftwater teams, sandbag crews
